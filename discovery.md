# Mode: discovery — Discovery Call Prep

Read `modes/_shared.md`, `modes/_profile.md`, `agency.md`, and `case-studies.md` before starting.

## Trigger
User preps for a discovery call or first meeting with a prospect.

## Process

### Step 1: Load Context
Read evaluation report from `reports/`. Web search for any recent news about the company, key people, or sector developments.

### Step 2: Generate Discovery Brief
Create `discovery-prep/{company-slug}-{YYYY-MM-DD}.md` with:

**Pre-Call Research**
- Company overview (what they do, how long, size)
- Current digital presence and its weaknesses
- Key people on the call and their backgrounds
- Recent news or developments
- Competitor context (who else might they be talking to?)

**Discovery Questions (10–15)**
Organised in order:
1. Rapport/context questions (2–3)
2. Problem/pain questions (4–5)
3. Budget/priority questions (2–3)
4. Decision/timeline questions (2–3)
5. Closing/next-step question (1)

**MurphAgency Proof Points to Deploy**
2–3 specific case studies from `case-studies.md` most relevant to this prospect's sector/problem.

**Objection Responses**
Pre-prepared responses for the 3 most likely objections based on sector patterns.

**Red Flags to Watch**
Signs this prospect might not be a good fit (from `modes/_profile.md` deal-breakers).

**Proposed Next Step**
What you want to walk out of this call with (verbal agreement, signed proposal, follow-up meeting).

### Step 3: Add to story-bank
After the call, Mordecai should debrief here. Log key learnings to `discovery-prep/story-bank.md`.

## Story Bank Format
```markdown
## {Company} — {Date}
**Outcome:** {Discovery / Proposal / Lost / Won}
**Key insight:** {What you learned about this sector/client type}
**What worked:** {Questions or approaches that landed well}
**What didn't:** {What to avoid next time}
```
